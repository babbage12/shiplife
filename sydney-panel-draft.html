The Call

"You Do Play Piano, Right?"

My first contract on the Pacific Sun had ended. I was home for a month, waiting to hear if P&O would bring me back. During my last week at sea, I'd gotten word that they wanted me - but nobody said which ship, or what I'd be doing.

Then the phone rang.

It was my agent. P&O wanted me back in four weeks for dry dock in Singapore. A "new" addition to the fleet - the Pacific Jewel. But there was a catch.

They didn't want me as a guitarist this time. They wanted me in the newly revamped lounge called "The Orient Bar." At the piano.

"You do play piano, right?"- My Agent

Well, yeah. Kind of.

I had worked a piano bar back in 1984 when I was 18 years old. I didn't last longer than one summer. I play by ear - I knew I could accompany myself through all the Billy Joel and Elton John songs I would ever need.

But was I a pianist? Not really. Not yet.

Singapore

The Construction Zone

I boarded the Pacific Jewel in Singapore with about a month left of dry dock. The ship was a construction zone.

Pacific Jewel in dry dock. Workers on bicycles passing by the massive hull.

Contractors everywhere. People yelling and shouting in languages I didn't recognize. Lines a mile long in the cafeteria with project managers, construction workers, welders. We ate with them. Partied with them. Sweated through the hot, sticky Singapore November. Tripped over them.

The musicians became close immediately. We had a few chores during the day - nothing too bad - but we got away as often as we could. I became very familiar with the Singapore MRT and the malls.

Malls meant only two things: food and wifi.

But there was a huge sense of underlying worry. The days were long. We walked for miles and miles every day around the city, finding cool things to do and places to just hang.

The Preparation

Pounding the Keys

I started working in the Orient Bar until very late at night while the place was still a mess. All I needed was the piano.

The Orient Bar. The room that terrified me.

I had well over a hundred songs on my list. But I found I was falling back on only 30 of them. Those things I needed to correct before I would be ready for this.

There were two other piano players in our group - Ashley and Alexander. Ashley was our band master. Alex was a child prodigy with a style similar to Liberace. Both were incredibly supportive because I was nervous as hell.

The first time I saw that room - the elegant Orient Bar with its beautiful baby grand piano - my fear didn't go away. It grew. But I was with the Food & Beverage Manager who was showing me around. I couldn't show it.

It may have only been my second contract at sea, but I had learned the importance of chain of command months ago.

The Stakes

Oh Holy Shit

The Jewel was doing longer cruises than the Sun. Sometimes we were going to be out for 18 days on this ship.

This was the inaugural cruise.

There would be dignitaries on board.

The governor.

Oh holy shit.

Yeah. I was nervous. Would you be nervous?

Darling Harbour

The Red Carpet

December 12th, 2009. Darling Harbour, Sydney.

I felt like a movie star. I was nervous as hell.

The inaugural party was that night. Pink Hummer limos. Crowds everywhere. Fireworks over Darling Harbour.

The inaugural party. Pink limos, stage setup, and later that night - the deck party with hundreds of people and Sydney glowing behind us.

I was either going to sink or swim in the following days.

Sailing Out

The Opera House at Night

We pulled away from the dock. The Opera House slid past, lit up against the night sky.

The Opera House through a porthole, and later - sliding past as we sailed into the unknown.

Somewhere in that harbor, the guitarist I used to be stayed behind.

The pianist I was about to become sailed out into the dark.

The Transformation

Sink or Swim

I don't remember the first night at that piano. I've blocked most of it out.

But I remember the second night. And the third. And somewhere around the fifth or sixth night, I realized something:

I wasn't drowning. I was swimming.

The ship had been transformed in Singapore. Rebuilt from the hull up.

So had I.
