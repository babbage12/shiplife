‚è∫ You're right about the manual date setting on older 
  cameras. Here's how it worked:                      
                                                      
  The date comes directly from the camera's internal  
  clock - there's no other source. When you press the 
  shutter, the camera writes its current date/time    
  into the EXIF metadata embedded in the image file.  
                                                      
  So yes, you must have had the wrong date set on the 
  camera. Common reasons this happened:               
                                                      
  1. Never set it properly - left it at factory       
  default when you first got it                       
  2. Internal battery died - many cameras had a small 
  backup battery just for the clock. When it died, the
   date would reset (often to something like Jan 1,   
  2009 or the manufacture date)                       
  3. Set the wrong year - easy to do when scrolling   
  through menus, accidentally picking 2009 instead of 
  2010                                                
  4. Time zone confusion - though that wouldn't       
  explain being off by 15+ months                     
                                                      
  The Panasonic DMC-FP8 came out in 2009, so if you   
  bought it new around then, you might have set it up 
  once and then the backup battery died later,        
  resetting it to a default date.                     
                                                      
  No GPS, no internet time sync, no automatic anything
   - just whatever you told it the date was. The good 
  old days!
